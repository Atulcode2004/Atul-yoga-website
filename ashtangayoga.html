Ashtanga Yoga

Dynamic and Structured Practice for the Mind and Body

What is Ashtanga Yoga?

Ashtanga Yoga is a vigorous style of yoga that follows a specific sequence of postures and links them with breath. The practice is highly structured and is ideal for those looking to increase strength, flexibility, and focus through a physically demanding practice.

Benefits of Ashtanga Yoga

  • Improves strength and flexibility
  • Boosts cardiovascular health
  • Enhances mental clarity and focus
  • Detoxifies the body through sweat and movement
  • Improves posture and alignment

Ashtanga Yoga Techniques

The Ashtanga practice involves a series of dynamic asanas (postures) practiced in a specific sequence with controlled breathing (Ujjayi breath). Some common poses in the primary series include:

  • Sun Salutations (Surya Namaskar A & B)
  • Standing Poses (e.g., Trikonasana, Parsvakonasana)
  • Seated Poses (e.g., Paschimottanasana, Marichyasana)
  • Backbends (e.g., Urdhva Dhanurasana)
  • Finishing Poses (e.g., Sarvangasana, Savasana)

Who Should Practice Ashtanga Yoga?

Ashtanga Yoga is ideal for those who enjoy a challenging, fast-paced practice that improves strength, flexibility, and endurance. It is recommended for practitioners who are already familiar with basic yoga poses and who wish to build a deeper and more consistent practice.
