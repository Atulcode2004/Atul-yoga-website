Restorative Yoga

Relax, Restore, and Rejuvenate

What is Restorative Yoga?

Restorative Yoga is a deeply relaxing and healing practice that focuses on slowing down the body and calming the mind. Using props such as blocks, blankets, and bolsters, this practice allows the body to fully relax and release tension.

Benefits of Restorative Yoga

  • Relieves chronic stress and anxiety
  • Improves sleep quality and relaxation
  • Increases flexibility and joint mobility
  • Reduces muscle tension and promotes healing
  • Boosts emotional balance and mental clarity

Restorative Yoga Techniques

Restorative Yoga involves long-held, supported poses that allow the body to completely relax. The most common poses include:

  • Supported Child's Pose (Balasana)
  • Reclining Butterfly Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana)
  • Supported Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)
  • Legs Up the Wall Pose (Viparita Karani)
  • Supported Savasana (Corpse Pose)

Who Should Practice Restorative Yoga?

Restorative Yoga is ideal for anyone who is looking to reduce stress and anxiety or those recovering from an injury. It is especially beneficial for beginners, seniors, or individuals who are looking for a relaxing and gentle approach to yoga.
